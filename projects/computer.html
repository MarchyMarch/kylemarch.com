My Personal Computer

List of Parts

CPU: Intel Core i7-6700k
CPU Cooler: Corsair H100i v2 Liquid Cooler
Motherboard: Asus Pro Gaming Mini ITX
Memory: G.Skill Ripjaws V Series 16GB DDR4-3000
Storage: Samsung 850 EVO 500GB M.2 SSD
GPU: MSI GeForce GTX 1070 8GB
Case: Phanteks Enthoo EVOLV Mini ITX
Power Supply: EVGA SuperNova 650W 80+ Gold Fully-Modular
Case Fan: Corsair ML120 Pro LED 120mm

The Installation Starts

I started off the installation by adding the SSD to the motherboard. It was an easy place for me to start because I have had experience with swapping out storage drives doing computer support at my university. I had some friends who said that they would help me but were busy when I began assembly, so needless to say, I was a little nervous doing this on my own.

Seated the CPU

So now for the scary part... at least I thought. My roommate said that seating the CPU is the scariest part but thankfully my motherboard had a tool to help in properly seating the CPU. I clamped it in and it felt amazing, so of course I took some candid shots. Now I was ready to go.

Adding More Parts

I started to add additional parts. I added in the RAM, which looks really nice with the motherboard. I also mounted and plugged in the GPU and case fan. My computer was starting to look like a real computer.

Fully Assembled

After figuring out where to plug everything in, I added in the liquid cooling. So now everything was fully put together and ready for the test drive. I got it hooked up to a monitor and flipped on the power supply and it was looking good, but as all programmers know, nothing works on the first try. I pressed the power button and nothing. I checked to see if the power button was going to the correct port, it was. To make a long story short, I tested every component of my computer and after 6 hours of testing I thought it had to be the motherboard. I began to take everything apart and noticed that I had plugged the wrong power cable into the CPU power slot...

IT'S ALIVE!!!

So needless to say, I found the problem. I had confused two of the power cables, both have the same plug ins but different pin sets. After that I plugged in the correct cable and pressed the power button and everything started to light up and whirring. I was pumped. Even though it was late and I had been troubleshooting for several hours I was way too excited to quit.

Booting Up

I got my computer hooked up to my monitor and was greeted by a BIOS screen. I was happy that I was able to get it working and I started my install of Windows.
